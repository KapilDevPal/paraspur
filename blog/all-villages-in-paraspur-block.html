Understanding the Paraspur Block Layout

Paraspur and its surrounding block is the largest administrative unit in the Gonda district of Uttar Pradesh. With a rich agricultural heritage and a growing infrastructure, the block is home to 91 distinct villages, each with its unique character, history, and community.

In this comprehensive guide, we've listed every single village within the Paraspur block. Whether you are a researcher, a local resident looking for information, or a business seeking to understand the local geography, this list serves as your definitive starting point.

Administrative Overview

The Paraspur block headquarters is located in the main Paraspur market area. The block is governed through its 64 Gram Panchayats. According to the 2011 Census, the total population of the block exceeds 2 lakhs, with a density that reflects the fertile Tarai soil of the region.

Development and Infrastructure

Over the last few years, the villages in the Paraspur block have seen significant improvements in road connectivity, particularly under the Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY). Most villages are now accessible via all-weather roads connecting them to Gonda and Lucknow.

Electrification is also widespread, with most hamlets receiving a steady supply of power compared to previous decades. The local economy remains primarily agricultural, with crops like wheat, rice, sugarcane, and mustard being the main produce.

Educational Hubs

While every major village has a primary or upper-primary school, certain villages like Pasaka and Paraspur act as educational hubs for the entire block. Institutions like the Tulsi Smarak Inter College have been educating the youth of these villages for generations, producing many scholars and civil servants.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Which is the largest village in Paraspur block?

By population and administrative importance, Paraspur itself is the largest settlement, followed by important hubs like Pasaka and Sakraur.

What is the total number of Gram Panchayats?

There are 64 Gram Panchayats that manage the 91 villages within the Paraspur block boundary.
