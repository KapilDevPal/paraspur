Must-visit historical and cultural landmarks in Lucknow

Bara Imambara

Architectural Marvel & Labyrinth

The central hall is one of the largest arched constructions in the world without any supporting pillars or beams.

Built in 1838 by Muhammad Ali Shah. Adorned with beautiful chandeliers and calligraphic inscriptions.

🛕

Chhota Imambara

Palace of Lights

Known as the Palace of Lights due to its elaborate chandeliers and glass decorations during festivals.

An imposing gateway built in 1784, representing classical Awadhi architecture. Modelled after the Sublime Porte in Istanbul.

Rumi Darwaza

Turkish Gate

A grand, sixty-foot gateway that has become the iconic symbol of Lucknow's classical identity.

The ruins of the British Residency complex, which served as a focal stage for the Siege of Lucknow in 1857.

British Residency

1857 Memorial Ruins

Preserved ruins displaying bullet and cannon-ball marks from the intense fighting of the First War of Independence.
