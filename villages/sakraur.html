Sakraur: A Spiritual & Agricultural Gem in Gonda

Discovering the unique geographical and cultural identity of Sakraur village in the Colonelganj region.

Introduction to Sakraur Village

Sakraur is a prominent village located in the Colonelganj taluka of the Gonda district, Uttar Pradesh. Also forming an integral part of the Paraspur Block, Sakraur is known for its vast geographical area and its spiritual significance. Surrounded by lush green fields and connected by the shared history of the Awadh region, Sakraur is a place where tradition and rural enterprise go hand in hand.

Geography & Land Use

One of the most defining characteristics of Sakraur is its size. The village covers a total geographical area of 832.3 Hectares (8.32 KM²), making it one of the larger villages in the block by landmass.

The terrain is predominantly flat, typical of the Indo-Gangetic plains, and is highly suitable for the cultivation of rice, wheat, and pulses. The large land area provides ample space for residential clusters, traditional grazing grounds, and expansive agricultural plots.

Population & Demographics

Sakraur is a well-established community with a balanced demographic profile. As per the 2011 Census, the village has seen steady growth over the decades.

With approximately 733 houses, the village maintains a tight-knit social structure where community festivals and village gatherings (Panchayats) are central to daily life.

Literacy & Social Indicators

The literacy rate in Sakraur is recorded at 42.9%. While there is a strong focus on educating the youth, the village faces challenges in terms of female literacy, which stood at 16.2% in the last census.

To address this, several local initiatives and government-run primary schools (Prathmik Vidyalaya) are working to increase enrollment. The proximity to higher-level schools in Paraspur also provides an avenue for advanced learning for the children of Sakraur.

The Shri Hanuman Temple

Sakraur holds a special place in the hearts of devotees due to the presence of the Shri Hanuman Temple. This temple is a major spiritual landmark, not just for the residents of Sakraur but also for people from neighboring villages like Pasaka (3 km away) and Durauni.

Every Tuesday and Saturday, the temple resonates with the chanting of the Hanuman Chalisa. During festivals like Hanuman Jayanti and Bada Mangal, the village transforms into a pilgrimage site, hosting local fairs (Melas) and community feasts (Bhandaras).

Economy & Infrastructure

The economy of Sakraur is almost entirely dependent on agriculture. The village has a well-developed network of irrigation canals and tube wells that support multiple cropping seasons. In recent years, infrastructure improvements under various rural development schemes have brought better road connectivity and improved power supply to the village.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Where is Sakraur village located?

Sakraur is located in the Colonelganj/Paraspur region of the Gonda district, Uttar Pradesh. It is easily accessible from the Gonda-Paraspur road.

What is the significance of the Shri Hanuman Temple in Sakraur?

The temple is a prominent spiritual center for the local community, attracting devotees from all over the Paraspur block, especially on auspicious days like Tuesday and Saturday.

What is the population of Sakraur?

Based on the 2011 Census, the population of Sakraur is 4,559 people living in about 733 households.

Summary

From its sprawling agricultural fields to its sacred temples, Sakraur is a village that beautifully preserves the traditional Awadhi lifestyle while embracing the possibilities of modern rural development. Its large land area and spiritual significance make it a standout location in the Paraspur block.
