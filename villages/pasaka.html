Pasaka: A Historic Hub in Paraspur Block

Exploring the demographics, culture, and community of one of Gonda's most populous villages.

Introduction to Pasaka Village

Located in the heart of the Paraspur Block within the Gonda district, Pasaka stands as a testament to the vibrant rural life of Uttar Pradesh. As one of the most populous villages in the region, Pasaka plays a vital role in the social and economic fabric of the block. Its proximity to the main market area of Paraspur and its large residential base make it a key focal point for local development initiatives.

Population & Demographics

Pasaka is characterized by its large community and significant residential footprint. According to the 2011 Census data, the village supports a substantial number of households.

16,069 Total Population
8,566 Males
7,503 Females

With over 2,473 houses, Pasaka is a bustling community. The gender ratio and the large child population indicate a young and growing demographic that requires consistent focus on education and healthcare.

Literacy & Education

The literacy challenge is a key focus area for Pasaka. As of the last official recording, the literacy rate was 42.54%.

  • Male Literacy: 50.26%
  • Female Literacy: 33.64%

While these numbers are below the state average, the presence of several primary and secondary schools in the vicinity (including those in neighboring Paraspur) is helping the younger generation bridge this gap. Students from Pasaka often attend colleges in Paraspur for higher studies.

The Sacred Confluence: Sukarkhet & Tulsi Das

Pasaka is home to one of the most spiritually significant sites in the Awadh region—Sukarkhet (also known as Paska Sangam). This is the holy confluence of the Sarayu and Ghaghara rivers, a place revered for centuries as a center of pilgrimage.

The most illustrious landmark here is the Goswami Tulsi Das Temple in Rajapur (adjacent to Paska). This site is traditionally recognized as the birthplace of the great saint Goswami Tulsidas, the author of the Ramcharitmanas. Every year, especially during the month of Kartik, thousands of devotees gather at the Sangam for a holy dip and to pay respects at the temple.

Historical Significance

Scholars often identify this area as the "Sukarkhet" mentioned in ancient texts where Tulsidas received spiritual guidance from his guru, Narharidas. The town of Rajapur in Gonda is thus an essential pillar of India's cultural and literary history.

Geography & Connectivity

Pasaka is well-connected to the rest of the Gonda district via a network of rural roads. It sits approximately 3 kilometers away from Sakraur and is a short drive from the Paraspur main market. The fertile agricultural land surrounding the village is the backbone of the local economy, with most residents engaged in farming activities.

Government Welfare & Development

The administration of Pasaka is managed by a dedicated Gram Panchayat. The village has been a beneficiary of various state and central schemes designed to improve rural infrastructure:

PM Awas Yojana (Rural)
Swachh Bharat Abhiyan
Jal Jeevan Mission
MGNREGA Employment

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the population of Pasaka village?

As per the 2011 Census, the population of Pasaka is approximately 16,069, making it one of the largest villages in the Paraspur block.

Which block is Pasaka located in?

Pasaka is located in the Paraspur Block of the Gonda district in Uttar Pradesh.

Are there schools in Pasaka?

Yes, Pasaka has primary government schools. For higher secondary and college education, students typically travel to the nearby Paraspur town.

Summary

Pasaka represents the true essence of rural Gonda—a large, tight-knit community with deep roots in agriculture and a growing focus on modern development. As road connectivity and digital access improve, Pasaka is poised to become an even more significant hub within the Paraspur block.
